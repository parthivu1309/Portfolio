First principles I live by

01

Build to understand

If I want to understand something deeply, I build something with it. Reading about pose estimation taught me nothing. Implementing it taught me everything.

02

Innovate in the question, not the answer

Most people copy solutions. I try to find better problems. Cric Intelligence started as "why can't I get coaching feedback at 2am?"

03

Earn the shortcut

I don't take shortcuts I haven't earned. In CP, I solve brute force first. In life, I read the primary source before the summary.

04

Think in systems, not events

A bad submission in a contest is an event. The reasoning pattern that caused it is a system. Fix the system.

05

Stay uncomfortably curious

Game theory, cricket physics, habit formation, ancient power dynamics — I follow curiosity wherever it goes, even when it seems unrelated. The connections always show up later.
